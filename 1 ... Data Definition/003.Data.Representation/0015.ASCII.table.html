Control Characters (0-31): The Silent Operators

These characters are like the secret handshakes of computers. They don't show up on your screen as text, but they tell your devices what to doâ€”things like moving the cursor, starting a new line, or making a sound. They're the unsung heroes of digital communication!

Decimal Hex Octal Symbol Description
0 00 000 NUL Null character: Like a blank slate, often used to indicate the end of a string.
1 01 001 SOH Start of Header: Marks the beginning of a message header.
2 02 002 STX Start of Text: Indicates the start of text in a message.
3 03 003 ETX End of Text: Signals the end of text.
4 04 004 EOT End of Transmission: Ends a transmission session.
5 05 005 ENQ Enquiry: Asks for a response from another device.
6 06 006 ACK Acknowledge: Confirms receipt of data.
7 07 007 BEL Bell: Makes a sound, like a digital "ding!".
8 08 010 BS Backspace: Moves the cursor back one position.
9 09 011 HT Horizontal Tab: Moves the cursor to the next tab stop.
10 0A 012 LF Line Feed: Moves the cursor to the next line.
11 0B 013 VT Vertical Tab: Moves the cursor down one line (vertical tab stop).
12 0C 014 FF Form Feed: Moves to the next page or form.
13 0D 015 CR Carriage Return: Moves the cursor to the beginning of the current line.
14 0E 016 SO Shift Out: Activates a special character set.
15 0F 017 SI Shift In: Deactivates a special character set.
16 10 020 DLE Data Link Escape: Changes the meaning of a limited number of following characters.
17 11 021 DC1 Device Control 1 (XON): Turns on a device.
18 12 022 DC2 Device Control 2: General purpose device control.
19 13 023 DC3 Device Control 3 (XOFF): Turns off a device.
20 14 024 DC4 Device Control 4: General purpose device control.
21 15 025 NAK Negative Acknowledge: Indicates data was not received correctly.
22 16 026 SYN Synchronous Idle: Used to establish or maintain synchronization.
23 17 027 ETB End of Transmission Block: Ends a block of data for transmission.
24 18 030 CAN Cancel: Indicates that the preceding data is in error and should be discarded.
25 19 031 EM End of Medium: Signals the end of a data medium (e.g., tape).
26 1A 032 SUB Substitute: Used in place of a character that is recognized to be in error.
27 1B 033 ESC Escape: Alters the meaning of the characters following it.
28 1C 034 FS File Separator: Separates files.
29 1D 035 GS Group Separator: Separates groups of information.
30 1E 036 RS Record Separator: Separates records.
31 1F 037 US Unit Separator: Separates units of information within a record.
