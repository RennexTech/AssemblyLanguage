ASCII (Dec) Hex Value Meaning (What it does) Mnemonic (Short Code) CTRL- Key Combo
000hNull: Like a blank slate or an empty envelope. Often used as a terminator.NULCtrl-@
101hStart of Heading: Signals the beginning of a message header. Think of it as "Chapter 1 Starts Here."SOHCtrl-A
202hStart of Text: Marks where the actual content of a message begins. "Okay, now for the main story."STXCtrl-B
303hEnd of Text: Signals the end of a message. Like saying "The End" at the end of a book.ETXCtrl-C
404hEnd of Transmission: Indicates the end of a data stream or transmission. "Data transfer complete!"EOTCtrl-D
505hEnquiry: Used to request a response from another device. "Are you there? Over."ENQCtrl-E
606hAcknowledge: Confirms that data has been received correctly. "Got it! Thanks!"ACKCtrl-F
707hBell: Triggers an audible bell or beep sound. "DING! Pay attention!"BELCtrl-G
808hBackspace: Moves the cursor one position back, deleting the character. "Oops, undo that!"BSCtrl-H
909hHorizontal Tab: Moves the cursor to the next tab stop. Like hitting the 'Tab' key.HTCtrl-I
100AhLine Feed: Moves the cursor to the next line without returning to the beginning. "Just go down one line."LFCtrl-J
110BhVertical Tab: Moves the cursor to the next vertical tab stop. Less common, but like a vertical 'Tab'.VTCtrl-K
120ChForm Feed: Advances the printer to the next page or the screen to clear. "New page, please!"FFCtrl-L
130DhCarriage Return: Moves the cursor to the beginning of the current line. "Go back to the start of this line!"CRCtrl-M
140EhShift Out: Switches to an alternative character set. "Change language settings!"SOCtrl-N
150FhShift In: Switches back to the standard character set. "Back to default language!"SICtrl-O
1610hData Link Escape: Changes the meaning of a limited number of following characters. "Escape sequence initiated!"DLECtrl-P
1711hDevice Control 1 (XON): Used to turn on a device or resume data flow. "Device, you're ON!"DC1Ctrl-Q
1812hDevice Control 2: General purpose device control. "Device, do something specific!"DC2Ctrl-R
1913hDevice Control 3 (XOFF): Used to turn off a device or pause data flow. "Device, you're OFF!"DC3Ctrl-S
2014hDevice Control 4: General purpose device control. "Device, do another specific thing!"DC4Ctrl-T
2115hNegative Acknowledge: Indicates data was received incorrectly or not at all. "Nope, try again!"NAKCtrl-U
2216hSynchronous Idle: Used to provide a signal in synchronous data transmission systems. "Waiting for sync..."SYNCtrl-V
2317hEnd of Transmission Block: Indicates the end of a block of data. "Block over!"ETBCtrl-W
2418hCancel: Indicates that the preceding data is to be disregarded. "Forget that last thing I said!"CANCtrl-X
2519hEnd of Medium: Indicates the physical end of a data medium. "Reached the end of the tape/disk!"EMCtrl-Y
261AhSubstitute: Used to replace a character that is in error or cannot be represented. "Replace with a placeholder."SUBCtrl-Z
271BhEscape: Used to provide an escape sequence for general-purpose extensions. "Special command coming up!"ESCCtrl-[
281ChFile Separator: Used to separate files. "Next file, please."FSCtrl-\
291DhGroup Separator: Used to separate groups of information. "New group of data."GSCtrl-]
301EhRecord Separator: Used to separate records. "Next record!"RSCtrl-^
311FhUnit Separator: Used to separate units of information. "Next unit!"USCtrl-_
