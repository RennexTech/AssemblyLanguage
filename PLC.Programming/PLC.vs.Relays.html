Control Method Hard-wired electromechanical logic. Physical connections determine control flow. Think of it like a fixed network of train tracks. Software-based programmable logic. Control flow determined by code. Like a digital train dispatcher that can change routes instantly.
Flexibility/Changes Low. Requires physical rewiring for *any* change. Imagine rebuilding a LEGO castle just to move one brick. High. Changes made via software programming. Just update the code! Like updating an app on your phone.
Complexity (Scaling) Becomes a "rat's nest" for complex systems; exponentially more relays & wires. Each new function adds a physical mess. Handles complex logic easily; scales efficiently with software. You add lines of code, not physical components.
Troubleshooting Difficult and time-consuming; need to trace physical wires, find faulty components in a tangle. Like finding a specific thread in a giant, knotted ball of yarn. Easier with diagnostic tools, software indicators, and less physical wiring. The PLC tells you exactly where the problem is.
Maintenance High. Mechanical parts wear out (coils, contacts); frequent replacements needed. Like constantly fixing a car with old parts. Low. Solid-state components are more reliable and last longer. Less physical wear and tear means less fixing.
Physical Space Requires large, often multiple, electrical cabinets to house many relays and wiring. A real space hog! Much more compact; integrates many functions into a small unit. Saves valuable factory floor space.
Cost (Initial) Lower for *very simple, basic* systems (fewer components). Higher for *very simple* systems due to the CPU's upfront cost.
Cost (Long-term) Higher due to extensive maintenance, lengthy troubleshooting time, and costly modifications. Lower due to reduced maintenance, easier modifications, and efficient diagnostics. Pays for itself over time.
Speed Slower, limited by mechanical switching time (milliseconds to seconds). Extremely fast (microseconds to milliseconds); limited by scan time. Processes information almost instantly.
Data Handling Limited to simple ON/OFF logic; no real data processing, storage, or communication capability. Can process, store, and communicate various types of data (e.g., analog values, precise counters, timers, network data). It's a data hub!
Diagnostic Capability Minimal, relies on external testers, multimeter, and visual inspection. You're mostly guessing. Built-in diagnostics, fault indicators, and logging capabilities. It tells you what's wrong, like a car's check engine light.
Application Very simple, fixed control tasks (rarely used for new designs today, mostly legacy systems). Nearly all industrial automation, from simple machines (like vending machines) to complex, large-scale processes (like entire car assembly lines).
